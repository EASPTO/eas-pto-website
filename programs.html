EAS Programs

From camping adventures to community stewardship, EAS offers experiences that go far beyond the classroom.

Wednesday Electives

🎨 Try Something New

Wednesday Elective sessions run five to six weeks and give students the opportunity to explore a new interest or skill for a limited time. Sessions change each semester, offering fresh options throughout the year.

All elective payments are processed through the EAS PTO.

Pay for Electives

Camps & Adventures

🏕️ Three Weeks of Adventure Each Year

EAS students attend three one-week camping and adventure experiences each school year. These trips are a cornerstone of the EAS experience, building teamwork, resilience, and a love of the outdoors.

Proper gear is key to a great trip experience.

Learn More on School Site 🎒 Gear Recommendations

Community Stewardship Projects

🌿 Giving Back to the Community

All EAS students participate in two 4-day blocks of Community Stewardship Projects (CSP) during the school year. A variety of projects gives students opportunities for active involvement in both environmental and social stewardship in their community.

CSPs are a unique aspect of EAS that reflect our school's values of environmental awareness and community care.

Health Fair

💚 Three Days of Health & Wellness

The EAS Health Fair is an enriching 3-day conference and one of the key ways district and state Health Curriculum guidelines are met. It brings in community experts and covers a wide range of health and wellness topics relevant to middle schoolers.

Interested in volunteering at the Health Fair? We'd love the help.

Staff Appreciation

💛 Celebrating Our Amazing Staff

During the school year, PTO supports "Monthly Meals" for our incredible EAS staff. We also host a cookie exchange before winter break and celebrate our teachers on or around National Teacher Appreciation Week in May.

Want to help treat our staff? There are many ways to pitch in.

Events

🎉 Building Community Together

PTO hosts the EAS End of Year Celebration, the 8th grade dinner/dance, and 8th grade graduation. We also organize structured family events throughout the year designed to build connection between our families.

These events make EAS the community it is — and they wouldn't happen without volunteers.
